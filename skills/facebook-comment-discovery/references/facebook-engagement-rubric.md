# Facebook Engagement Rubric

Use this after ranking comments.

## Engagement modes

### 1. Ignore

Use when:

- the comment is praise only
- the signal is too weak
- the thread is mostly fandom or social noise
- a reply would look opportunistic

### 2. Monitor

Use when:

- the profile cluster is strong
- the comment is interesting but too shallow for a credible reply
- the commenter may be worth revisiting later

### 3. Reply in thread

Use when:

- the commenter asked a direct question
- the commenter exposed a real workflow problem
- you can answer without hijacking the creator thread
- the reply would still be useful if the offer did not exist

### 4. Profile follow-up

Use when:

- the commenter looks relevant but the thread is not the right place
- you want to inspect the profile for more fit clues
- the account should be kept only as a seed source, not as current goodput

## Rules of thumb

- Big Facebook creator threads are easy to spam. Be strict.
- If the reply mainly serves your visibility and not the commenter, skip it.
- Short helpful replies beat mini-frameworks.
- If the thread is aesthetic, celebratory, or fandom-heavy, avoid forcing a
  consulting tone into it.
